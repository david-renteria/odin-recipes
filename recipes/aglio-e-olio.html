Aglio e Olio Pasta

Description

"Aglio e Olio" means "olive oil and garlic" in Italian, and is a traditional pasta recipe from Naples. This dish is all about the simplicity of the ingredients and is very easy and quick to make.

Ingredients

  • 16 oz | linguini (uncooked)
  • 0.375 cups | olive oil
  • 1 tsp | red pepper flakes (or more if you like spicy food)
  • 5-6 cloves | garlic
  • 0.5 tsp | salt (or to taste)
  • 0.5 tsp | black pepper (or to taste)
  • 0.5 cup | fresh chopped Italian parsley
  • 3 tbsp | lemon juice (about one lemon)

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil and heavily salt it.
  2. Add pasta and cook until al dente.
  3. Heat olive oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  4. Once oil is simmering, add red pepper flakes and garlic, stirring constantly to avoid burning.
  5. Once garlic is slightly golden brown, remove from heat and set aside until pasta is finished cooking.
  6. Before draining pasta, remove some water and set aside.
  7. Drain pasta and immediately add to pan with oil-garlic-red pepper mix.
  8. Add parsley, black pepper, and salt, then toss over low-medium heat.
  9. If pasta is too dry, add some of the pasta water that was set aside.
  10. Squeeze lemon juice into pan, then mix up a little more.
  11. The pasta is now ready to serve.
  12. (optional) Garnish plate with freshly grated parmesan.
